Airline Route Fare & Demand Analytics

  • Built a consulting-style demo for American Airlines: cleaned a 640-route dataset by removing 2 corrupted rows, researching 6 missing distance values, and eliminating 4 duplicate routes down to a validated 634-route dataset.
  • Compared multiple predictive approaches for passenger volume — 4 linear regression specifications, a cost-complexity-pruned regression tree, and 5 random forest configurations — evaluating each on RMSE, mean error, and R² to identify the best-performing model.
  • Formulated a binary integer program in R (lpSolve) to select the 23 highest revenue-per-route cities to prioritize for network expansion, maximizing projected route efficiency under a city-count constraint.

Monte Carlo Inventory-Risk Simulation

  • Built an @RISK model that sizes a single-season inventory order under uncertain demand and quantifies the resulting profit distribution.
  • Models demand as a PERT distribution (minimum 50, most likely 150, maximum 325) for a newsvendor-style ordering decision.
  • Sweeps candidate order quantities with RiskSimtable and charges a penalty cost for reserved units that go unsold, outputting mean profit, 1st and 99th percentiles, probability of a loss, and probability of clearing a target profit.
